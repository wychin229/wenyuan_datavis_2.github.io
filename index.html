History of Olympics



Since the opening of the first modern Olympic Games in 1896, the international sports competition has only been canceled 3 times: once during World War I (1916) and twice during World War II (1940, 1944).[2]

Athletes Gender Equality

According to The Conversation's article[4], The Internation Olympic Committee has called the Tokyo Olympics the most gender equal Games of all time, with women comprising a record-breaking 49 percent of participants.

Below the visualisation shows the population pyramid according to athlete's gender in all of the events in the Tokyo Olympics 2020.
We can see that the gender distribution is quite average across the two gender with overall men at 51% and women at 49%. This is Tokyo Olympics 2020 has added 18 new events and established equal number of spots for both men and women. Other than that, there is also a higher number of mixed-gender competitions.

Figures above visualises the gender distribution of athletes in Olympics in 1968, 1984, 2000 and 2016.

From the above pie charts, we can see that there is a significant increase in female athletes from 1980s to date. This is due to the efforts of the International Olympic Committee (IOC)[5] in order to achieve gender equality so that girls and women around the world are given greater access and opportunities to participate in sport events.

Medals

Tokyo Olympics 2020 committee had initiated their Tokyo 2020 Medal Project[6] to collect small electronic devices such as used mobile phones from all over Japan to produce the Olympic and Paralympic medals. According to them, approximate 78,985 tons of material was collected, including approximate 6.21 million of used mobile phones which was used to produce approximate 5,000 medals (gold, silver, bronze).
