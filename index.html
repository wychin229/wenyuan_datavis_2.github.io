The Olympics Logo

The 5 rings are intended to represents the five continents: Europe, Africa, Asia, the Americas and Oceania. Other than that, the six colours including the white background are combined this way to reproduce the colours of every country without exception. [1]


History of Olympics



Since the opening of the first modern Olympic Games in 1896, the international sports competition has only been canceled 3 times: once during World War I (1916) and twice during World War II (1940, 1944).[2]

Hence, in the line chart below where it shows the number of athletes participated in each Olympics games, there is no record for these 3 games.

The below visualisation visualises the number of athletes in past Olympic Games from the Top 5 countries which won the most medal in Tokyo Olympics 2020, which are the United States of America (USA) , China (CHN), Japan (JPN), the United Kingdoms (GBR) and Russian Olympic Committee (RUS). [3]

Athletes Gender Equality

According to The Conversation's article[4], The Internation Olympic Committee has called the Tokyo Olympics the most gender equal Games of all time, with women comprising a record-breaking 49 percent of participants.

Below the visualisation shows the population pyramid according to athlete's gender in all of the events in the Tokyo Olympics 2020. We can observe that the gender distribution is quite average across the two gender with overall men at 51% and women at 49%.
