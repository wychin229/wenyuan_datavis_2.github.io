The Olympics Logo

The 5 rings are intended to represents the five continents: Europe, Africa, Asia, the Americas and Oceania.[1]


120 Years of Olympic Games

As shown in the bar chart, there is no record for a few games. This is because since the opening of the first modern Olympic Games in 1896, the international sports competition has only been canceled 3 times: once during World War I (1916) and twice during World War II (1940, 1944).[2]

The below visualisation visualises the number of athletes in past Olympic Games from the Top 5 countries which won the most medal in Tokyo Olympics 2020, which are the United States of America (USA) , China (CHN), Japan (JPN), the United Kingdoms (GBR) and Russian Olympic Committee (RUS). [3]

Athletes Gender Equality

Below in the visualisation visualises the gender distribution of athletes in the respective game
(as of the year in chart title). We can observe that there is asignificant growth in number of female athletes
in the Olympic Games from past to more recent games.

According to The Conversation's article[5], The Internation Olympic Committee has called the Tokyo Olympics
the Most Gender Equal Game of all time, with women comprising a record-breaking 49 percent of participants.

The visualisation above shows the gender distribution of Tokyo Olympics 2020,
we can see that overall men are at 51% and women are at 49%.
